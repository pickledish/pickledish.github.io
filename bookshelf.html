I'm by no means a voracious reader, but I go through a book or two every few months, and over the years I've encountered a few that have really had an impact on how I view the world and conduct myself within it. I thought it might be nice to write this set of books down somewhere, such that if anyone wants to understand me a little better, I can point them here and heartily recommend everything on the list.

So -- here are some special books, in the order I read them:

The Stranger
Albert Camus
1942
Eating Animals
Jonathan Safran Foer
2009
Bullshit Jobs
David Graeber
2018
Debt: The First 5,000 Years
David Graeber
2011
Small is Beautiful
E. F. Schumacher
1973
